The Variability Selection Hypothesis

(Potts, 1998-2023)

Human evolution was driven by high climate fluctuation in East Africa.

We didn't adapt to one habitat. We adapted to unpredictability itself.

Result:

  • Bipedalism (flexibility)
  • Tool use (problem-solving)
  • Large brains (modeling uncertainty)
  • Culture (rapid adaptation without genetic change)
Humans are built for antifragility.
