UAP Falsifiers

Explicit Conditions That Would Break UAP

Purpose

A claim without falsification conditions is not scientific. This document specifies exactly what would falsify UAP.

The Four Falsifiers

F1: Stable Interaction Without Acknowledgment

Claim: All stable interaction requires some form of acknowledgment-gate. Falsifier: A protocol that achieves bidirectional stability over time without any coherence verification mechanism. What would count:
  • A communication protocol with no ACK, no checksum, no verification
  • That remains stable under noise, delay, and interference
  • Over extended time periods
Status: No example found. All stable protocols have acknowledgment.

F2: Forcing That Systematically Increases Coherence

Claim: Forcing (option-reduction without consent) degrades coherence. Falsifier: Systematic evidence that coercion improves mutual understanding (not just compliance). What would count:
  • Relationship studies showing forced interaction increases trust
  • Organizations where top-down mandates increase alignment
  • Learning studies where forced study improves comprehension
Note: Compliance ‚â† coherence. A prisoner "complies" but is not aligned. Status: No example found. Forcing creates compliance or resistance, not coherence.

F3: Simpler Irreducible Primitive

Claim: The monad M := (B ‚Üî D) is the minimal structure for interaction. Falsifier: A structure more minimal than B ‚Üî D that still supports interaction. What would count:
  • Distinction without any standpoint
  • Standpoint without any distinction
  • A primitive that contains neither but enables interaction
Status: No example found. INIT0 runs consistently terminate at B ‚Üî D.

F4: Stable Interaction Without Meta-Consent

Claim: Long-term stability requires meta-consent (consent to interact at all). Falsifier: An interaction that remains stable over time under forced engagement without any opt-out. What would count:
  • Mandatory relationships that stay genuinely aligned
  • Compulsory participation that creates real coherence
  • Long-term stability without any ability to exit
Status: No example found. Forced interactions show compliance, resentment, or eventual exit.

How To Test

For F1

1. Design a protocol without acknowledgment 2. Run it under realistic conditions 3. Measure stability over time 4. If stable: F1 is true, UAP falsified

For F2

1. Conduct controlled study 2. One group: forced interaction 3. One group: consensual interaction 4. Measure coherence (not just compliance) 5. If forced group has higher coherence: F2 is true, UAP falsified

For F3

1. Apply INIT0 from novel starting point 2. Reduce until irreducible 3. Compare result to M := (B ‚Üî D) 4. If simpler: F3 is true, UAP falsified

For F4

1. Study long-term forced interactions 2. Measure genuine alignment (not just compliance) 3. If stable alignment: F4 is true, UAP falsified
