Do I Understand You Correctly?

The Core of UAP in One Page

The Question

"Do I understand you correctly?"

This question is not a technique. It is not a method. It is not a tool.

It is the minimal form of stable interaction.

What the Question Does

When you ask "Do I understand you correctly?", you do three things:

1. You acknowledge that your interpretation might be wrong 2. You give space for the other to correct 3. You create consent for what follows

Without this question, every response is an assumption. With this question, every response is verified.

Why It Works

The question works because it:

  • Prevents forcing ‚Äî you don't push your interpretation as truth
  • Enables recovery ‚Äî misunderstandings are caught immediately
  • Builds coherence ‚Äî each step is confirmed

When To Use It

  • At the start of a conversation (meta-consent)
  • After you've listened and want to respond
  • When you sense drift or confusion
  • Before making decisions that affect others

When It Fails

The question fails when:

  • You ask it but don't listen to the answer
  • You use it to manipulate ("Don't you agree that...?")
  • You skip the meta-consent ("Do you want me to check?")

2. Listen
3. Summarize: "What I hear is..."
4. Check: "Do I understand you correctly?" (C‚ÇÅ)
5. If no: go to 2

That's it.

What This Is Not

  • Not a trick to win arguments
  • Not a way to seem empathetic
  • Not a manipulation technique
  • Not spiritual or mystical
It is simply the grammar of voluntary coherence.
