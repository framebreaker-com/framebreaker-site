The Most Important Question

When you talk to someone, there's one question that makes everything better:

"Do I understand you correctly?"

Why This Question Is Magic

When you ask this question, three things happen:

The Two Rules

Rule 1: Ask First

Before you start helping or giving advice, ask:

"Do you want to talk about this?"

Maybe the other person just wants to be heard. Maybe they want to figure it out themselves. That's okay!

Rule 2: Check Along the Way

After you listen, say what you understood:

"So what I hear is that you're sad because... Is that right?"

A Story

Without the question:

Emma: "I'm angry!" Dad: "Then you should go to your room until you calm down." Emma: "You don't understand me!" (runs away crying)

With the question:

Emma: "I'm angry!" Dad: "Sounds like something happened. Do you want to tell me about it?" Emma: "Yes..." Dad: "So you're angry because Tim took your toy. Did I understand that correctly?" Emma: "Yes! And then he lied about it!" Dad: "Ah, so it's also about the lying. That makes sense that you're extra angry." Emma: "Yes..." (feels understood)

The Secret

The secret is this:

You don't have to solve the problem.

You just have to understand it.

Often that's already enough.

Try It

Today, try with one person:

1. Ask if they want to tell you something 2. Listen without interrupting 3. Say what you understood 4. Ask: "Did I get that right?"

See what happens!
