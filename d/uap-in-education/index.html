The Insight-Based Lesson

Phase 1: Create Curiosity (Snapshot Trigger)

Don't start with answers. Start with questions.

TraditionalInsight-based
"Today we learn X""Here's a puzzle..."
"The formula is...""What do you notice?"
"The answer is...""What would you try?"
Goal: Destabilize existing frame (create the gap).

Phase 2: Hold Space (The Gap)

Allow confusion. Don't rush to resolve.

TraditionalInsight-based
Immediately explainWait
Fill silenceLet silence work
"The answer is...""What are you thinking?"
Goal: Protect the space where insight can occur.

Phase 3: Mirror (Support Self-Discovery)

When students articulate, reflect back.

TraditionalInsight-based
"No, the answer is...""So you're saying...?"
"That's wrong""What would that imply?"
"Let me explain again""Can you say more?"
Goal: Help students hear themselves.

Phase 4: Recognize Lock

When insight occurs, it's visible:

  • "Oh! I get it!"
  • Sudden confidence
  • Ability to explain to others
  • Transfer to new problems
Goal: Acknowledge without overdoing.

The Question in Education

"Do I understand you correctly?"

In education, this becomes:

"Do you understand me correctly?"

But even better:

"What do you understand?"

This invites self-observation. The student articulates their model. Gaps become visible. Insight becomes possible.

For Teachers: Practical Checklist

Before class:
  • [ ] What puzzle/question will create curiosity?
  • [ ] What assumptions might be destabilized?
During class:
  • [ ] Am I forcing or inviting?
  • [ ] Am I filling silence or holding space?
  • [ ] Am I explaining or mirroring?
  • [ ] Am I checking consent/engagement?
When students struggle:
  • [ ] Is this productive confusion (gap)?
  • [ ] Do they need more space or different space?
  • [ ] Am I patient enough?
When insight occurs:
  • [ ] Did I recognize it?
  • [ ] Did I let them own it?
  • [ ] Did I resist over-explaining?
