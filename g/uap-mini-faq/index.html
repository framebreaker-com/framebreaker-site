Frequently Asked Questions

Is this soft?

No. It only forbids coercion, not sharpness.

You can be completely clear, direct, and critical ‚Äî as long as you don't reduce the other's options without consent.

Is this slow?

Faster than escalation + repair.

One "Do I understand you correctly?" costs 5 seconds. Repairing one misunderstanding costs hours.

Is this moral?

No. It's structural.

UAP doesn't say what's good or bad. It only says: if you want interaction to remain stable, this works.

Is this spiritual?

Only if silence is spiritual for you ‚Äî but that's not in the spec.

UAP makes no claims about consciousness, soul, or transcendence. The notation ùïã is a stop sign, not a sanctuary.

Does this work with people who don't cooperate?

Partially.

UAP cannot force someone to participate. But it can:

  • Recognize forcing
  • Stop escalation
  • Keep your side clean

What if the other person lies?

Lying = forcing (you reduce the other's options through false information).

UAP doesn't detect this directly, but:

  • Coherence will drop
  • Recovery will trigger
  • The interaction will become unstable

Is this the same as nonviolent communication?

Related, but not identical.

NVC focuses on needs and feelings. UAP focuses on consent-gates and coherence.

They are complementary.

What if I disagree with UAP?

Fine.

UAP doesn't claim to be universal. It says: if you want stable interaction without coercion, then this works.

If you have different goals, UAP may not be for you.

What's the difference from "just listening"?

UAP is more precise:

  • Not just listening, but checking
  • Not just understanding, but confirming
  • Not just giving space, but asking consent
